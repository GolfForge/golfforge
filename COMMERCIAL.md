## Which license do I need?

**You can use the AGPL-3.0 (free) if** you are willing to comply with its copyleft terms — most
importantly:

- If you distribute the software or a modified version, you must make the **complete corresponding
  source** available under the AGPL.
- **AGPL section 13 (network use):** if you run a modified version that users interact with over a
  network (for example a hosted or multiplayer server), you must offer those users the source of
  your modified version.

This covers personal use, evaluation, research, internal tinkering, and any open-source project
that is itself willing to be AGPL.

**You need a commercial license if** you want to:

- ship the software (or a derivative) inside a **closed-source / proprietary** product,
- offer it as a hosted service **without** releasing your modifications under the AGPL, or
- otherwise use it in a way the AGPL's copyleft obligations don't permit.

## Contributions

So that the project can keep offering both licenses, external contributions are accepted under a
Contributor License Agreement (CLA) that grants the maintainer the rights needed to relicense
contributed code (including under the commercial license). Until the CLA process is in place,
external pull requests are not being merged — see `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

monitor talked to over a socket, etc. — remain under their respective licenses and are not
