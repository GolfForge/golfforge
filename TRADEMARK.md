"GolfForge", the GolfForge name, and the GolfForge logo (the "Marks") are trademarks of

The Marks are **separate from the software license.** The code is free and open under the
GNU AGPL-3.0 (plus a commercial option) — see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE) and
[`COMMERCIAL.md`](COMMERCIAL.md). Those licenses grant rights to the **code**; they do **not**
grant any right to use the Marks. Trademark use is governed by this policy and by trademark law.

## The short version

- ✅ Use the GolfForge name to **truthfully refer** to the project ("works with GolfForge",
  "based on GolfForge", tutorials, reviews, talks).
- ✅ Redistribute **unmodified** GolfForge under the GolfForge name.
- ❌ Ship a **modified version / fork** under the GolfForge name or logo — **give it your own
  name** (you may still say "based on GolfForge").
- ❌ Use the Marks in a way that implies official status, endorsement, or affiliation that
  doesn't exist.

## What you can do without asking

- Refer to GolfForge by name to describe, discuss, review, teach, or compare it.
- State accurately that your product, plugin, course, or service "works with", "is built on",
  or "is derived from" GolfForge.
- Redistribute official, **unmodified** GolfForge builds or sources under the GolfForge name.
- Use the name in community contexts (user groups, fan content, articles) as long as it's clear
  you don't speak for the project.

## Forks and modified versions — pick your own name

This is the core of the policy (the model used by Firefox, Rust, and many others):

- If you distribute a **modified** version of GolfForge — a fork, a re-skin, a patched build —
  you **must not** use "GolfForge" or the GolfForge logo as the name or primary branding of that
  distribution. **Choose a distinct name and logo.**
- You may, and should, **truthfully** state that your project is "based on GolfForge" or "a fork
  of GolfForge" in your description — just not as your product's name/brand.
- Don't use names or logos that are confusingly similar to "GolfForge".

The point is simple: anyone who downloads something called **GolfForge** should be getting the
genuine project, not a modified build with different behavior, security posture, or data handling.
The code stays 100% free to fork — only the name travels with the official project.

## Logo

- Don't alter, recolor, or distort the logo.
- Don't use the logo as the icon/branding for your own product or fork.
- Don't combine it with other logos in a way that implies a partnership.

## Please don't

- Imply endorsement, sponsorship, or official affiliation without written permission.
- Register domain names, social handles, app-store listings, or company/product names that use
  "GolfForge" in a way that suggests you are the official project.
- Use the Marks in misleading, disparaging, or unlawful ways.

## Questions and permission

This policy can't cover every case. If you want to use the Marks in a way that isn't clearly
allowed above — or you're not sure — just ask. Permission is often easy to grant for reasonable
community uses.
